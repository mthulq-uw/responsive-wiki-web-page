Section 2: The Churning Process

Gelato is churned at a slower speed than ice cream, which incorporates less air and creates that signature dense texture. Most home machines work fine, but try to churn it until it reaches a soft-serve consistency.

Popular Flavors

Classic Italian gelato shops typically offer flavors like stracciatella (chocolate chip), pistachio, hazelnut, and various fruit flavors. For fruit gelatos, use fresh, ripe fruit and blend it into a smooth puree before adding to your base.

Storage Tips

Store your gelato in a shallow, airtight container to minimize ice crystal formation. Unlike ice cream, gelato doesn't keep as long due to its lower fat content, so it's best consumed within a week or two for optimal texture and flavor.
